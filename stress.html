Six Tips for Managing Stress

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Relax your muscles one at a time, starting from your toes and moving up your body. This technique has been shown to reduce anxiety and stress hormones.

Mindful Breathing

Practice deep, slow inhalations and exhalations, focusing all your attention on your breath. This “reset” button can help calm your mind and body.

Prioritize Organization

Plan your day effectively to avoid feeling overwhelmed. Poor organization skills can lead to lost time and increased stress levels.

Identify and Challenge Negative Coping Mechanisms

Recognize unhealthy coping strategies, such as substance abuse or avoidance, and replace them with healthier alternatives. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you.

Prioritize Physical Health

Ensure you get enough restful sleep (6-8 hours) to help your body recover from stress. Listen to your body and adjust your sleep schedule accordingly.

Practice Self-Awareness and Perspective

Keep things in perspective, acknowledging that everyone has bad days. Don’t be too hard on yourself, and consider seeking professional help if needed.
