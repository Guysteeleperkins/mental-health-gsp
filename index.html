Common Issues and Tips

Anxiety and Panic attacks
  • Deep Breathing: Focus on slow, deep breaths, inhaling for 4 seconds, holding for 4 seconds, and exhaling for 4 seconds. This can help regulate breathing and reduce symptoms.
  • Grounding Techniques: Focus on your surroundings, noticing sights, sounds, and textures. This can help you feel more connected to reality and reduce feelings of detachment.
  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation: Tense and then relax different muscle groups, starting from your toes and moving up to your head. This can help release physical tension.
Anger
  • Identifying Triggers
    • Recognize your personal triggers, such as specific situations, people, or events, that can lead to anger.
    • Be aware of your physical warning signs, like a racing heart or tense muscles, that indicate your anger is building.
  • Managing Anger
    • Practice relaxation techniques, like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or meditation, to calm down quickly.
    • Engage in physical activity, such as exercise or yoga, to release tension and improve your mood.
Depression

  • Exercise for Mental Health: Exercise is a natural and effective way to reduce stress, improve mood, and alleviate symptoms of depression. It can also improve energy levels, balance, and flexibility.
  • Self-Care and Routine: Establishing a daily routine can help provide structure and stability, which can be beneficial for individuals struggling with depression. This can include activities such as:
    • Cooking and eating regular meals
    • Staying active, even if it’s just a short walk
    • Practicing mindfulness or meditation
    • Engaging in hobbies or creative activities

Loneliness
  • Talk to someone: Share your feelings with a trusted friend, family member, or healthcare professional. Talking can help you feel heard and understood.
  • Join a friendship group or club: Engage in activities that interest you and meet new people with similar passions.
  • Volunteer: Helping others can give you a sense of purpose and connection.
  • Take advantage of befriending services: Age UK’s Telephone Friendship Service, face-to-face befriending, and other local services can match you with someone to talk to.
  • Look after yourself: Prioritize your physical and mental well-being, as neglecting your own needs can exacerbate feelings of loneliness.
